## Previous Art and Influences

These standards stand on prior work including:

- **Asilomar AI Principles** (2017) – Beneficial AI, value alignment
- **EU Ethics Guidelines for Trustworthy AI** (2019) – Human agency, technical robustness, privacy, transparency, diversity, fairness, societal well-being, accountability
- **Google AI Principles** (2018) – Avoid creating or reinforcing bias, be socially beneficial, accountable to people
- **Microsoft Responsible AI Principles** (2018) – Fairness, reliability, privacy, inclusiveness, transparency, accountability
- **Partnership on AI** – Best practices for responsible AI development
- **IEEE Ethically Aligned Design** – Standardization of ethical considerations in autonomous systems

Unlike corporate or institution-specific principles, the Universal Standards are:
- **Non-proprietary** (no single entity controls them)
- **Implementation-oriented** (actionable requirements, not just aspirations)
- **Legally integrated** (explicit compliance pathways)
- **Open to all** (no membership, no fees, no licensing)

## Final Note

Standards alone do not ensure ethical AI. Implementation, enforcement, and cultural change within organizations are equally critical. These Universal Standards provide the minimum baseline; responsible actors must exceed them where possible.
