# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We, as contributors and maintainers of the Universal Standards for LLM-Based Assistants, are committed to fostering an inclusive, respectful, and harassment-free community for all participants, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual orientation.

## Expected Behavior

- **Respectful communication:** Treat all participants with dignity and consideration
- **Constructive criticism:** Focus on ideas, not individuals; provide feedback in good faith
- **Professional conduct:** Uphold the ethical standards we advocate in our own interactions
- **Inclusivity:** Welcome newcomers and diverse perspectives
- **Accountability:** Accept responsibility for your words and actions

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behaviors include, but are not limited to:
- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments
- Trolling, inflammatory, or off-topic comments that derail discussions
- Publishing private information (doxxing) without explicit permission
- Sexual attention or advances of any kind
- Threats of violence or encouragement of harm
- Any other conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:
- Correction (private or public, as appropriate)
- Temporary or permanent suspension of contribution privileges
- Removal from community channels
- Escalation to appropriate authorities for severe violations

Enforcement actions are at the discretion of the maintainers and will be communicated clearly.

## Reporting

If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior:
1. Document the incident (screenshots, timestamps, context)
2. Report privately to maintainers at: [INSERT CONTACT EMAIL]
3. For GitHub-specific issues, use the security disclosure process

All reports will be reviewed promptly and confidentially. Retaliation against reporters is strictly prohibited.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces (GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, mailing lists) and to public representations of the project (conferences, social media, etc.).
